Our Mission

Beauty
that sustains.

TULUM was founded to empower the textile artists of Osh — a region where the craft of knotting has been passed from mother to daughter for centuries. We create direct market access, fair wages, and international visibility for their work.



The word тулум (tulum) in Kyrgyz refers to the festive garment — an expression of dignity and community. Our rugs carry that spirit into modern spaces.

How it's made

From sheep
to room.

01 — RAW WOOL

Tian Shan Wool

Sourced directly from nomadic families in the Tian Shan mountains. No middlemen. The wool is washed after shearing and hand-spun on site.

02 — DYEING

Natural Pigments

Plant-based dyes from walnut shells, pomegranate and indigo. Our palette spans 12 tones — all reproducible, all permanent.

03 — DESIGN

Co-Creation

Every design emerges in dialogue: the artisans' traditional pattern archives are recalibrated with minimal grid thinking. No compromise on either side.

04 — KNOTTING

6 to 9 Weeks

Hand-knotted on vertical looms. 180,000 to 320,000 knots per rug. Each artisan signs her piece with a personal weaving mark.

Design Philosophy

Kyrgyz
geometry
meets USM.

USM Haller resolved the furniture system through a universal module grid — infinite scalability from a single connector logic. Kyrgyz textile art works identically: a binding module system of diamonds, spirals, and concentric forms. Endlessly scalable.

Our designs distil both systems. No historicising ornament. No empty modernism. Patterns that carry their origin without quoting it.
